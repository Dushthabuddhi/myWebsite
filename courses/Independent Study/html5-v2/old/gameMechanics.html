Sample Game Play

Crouching in the early dawn on top of a 20 story building looking down upon a hoard of Walking trying to force themselves into the building to quench their hunger for flesh. In your search to find supplies and fuel you have been forced to take refuge on top of a building to help others within you team to escape. You are accompanied by a Gangster who you come upon and rescue during your supply mission. You must find refuge soon as you are fully aware that when night comes you will also have to worry about the Dark Hunters that will be out and about in search for sport. However you are also aware of the fact the night time is something the best time to make your move as this is when the battle between the Walkers and the Dark Hunters begin.

Core Game Play / Game Flow

Game play in Death Event is fast-paced and action-packed. It combines the shoot-em-up, instant-gratification style of an arcade gun-blazing maelstrom with longer term rewards more usually associated with adventure games, including the acquisition of reputation and leadership gauge.

The pure action-elements form the core of the game play. Melee to mid-range combat with large, frenzied flocks of Walkers is the norm. Hesitation, ammo-conversation, slow and drawn out strategies are of no use here; your survival depends on being either very fast, or very strong. Intelligence will never be punished of course, and opportunities will arise where a keen and observant player will clearly have the advantage over the casual player; examples include specific Walker and Dark Hunter behaviors and weaknesses, vulnerabilities of certain Walkers and Dark Hunters to particular weapons or environmental conditions, as well as such standard tactical conundrums as "Who do I get rid of first?", given a mixed bag of Walkers, Corruptor and Dark Hunters around you from all directions.

The longer term, adventure-style elements of the game include the player's accumulated reputation points, acquired supplies and special, rare valuables, and the exploration and control of territory for safety. These aspects of the game allow the player an on-going campaign style of play, with elements of character-development, encouraging the player to reach new goals and levels of success, as well as fostering multiple styles of competition from others vying for your position as new people are rescued introduced to the community.

Reputation

Reputation is a core component of Death Event. Reputation can be gained through several methods. First of all, each Zomby-type will have a reputation value attached to it, which upon disposal of said zomby will be awarded to the player (some, if not most zombies will actually have very little reputation points; they are reserved for the more difficult, legendary of beasts). But a zombies's reputation score is not the final word on how much a player receives; indeed, the method, grace, and skill employed in a zombiess demise are all factored into points won, so that the truly inspired players gain extra bonus points, so to speak.

Reputation is also gained through the acquisition of rare valuables, notably essential supplies for survival, defense and creative execution. Scattered throughout the game will be various highly prized objects which will be added to a player's permanent collection, thereby increasing the player's overall reputation score.

The other method of gaining reputation is through proper management of tasks distribution when camping, infiltrating a mall or directing a convoy. See below.

Camping

Camping involves many small nuances with include gathering of resources such as water, wood and food growing in the wild to farming for food, washing cloths and defending the area from potential threats. Managing these tasks is the job of the leader to distribute. Each of these tasks will be portrayed through mini games which the user will also be able to play in an effort to earn reputation points and increase overall morality of the group you are managing. Mini games such as Plants Vs. Zombiesis a perfect example of how this is possible.
